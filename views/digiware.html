DIGI-WARE

  1. Home
  2. Blogs

The project is aimed to introduce Verilog programming to mentees. The project consisted of an extension of topics related to digital electronics, like flip-flops, MUX, etc., which were covered in the course ESC201. This course was done by Y22 mentees but not Y23s. So, the mentors decided to conduct the project separately for Y22s and Y23s. The general workflow consisted of going through the provided resources and attending discussion hours. Assignments were sent immediately after and the mentees were given 1-2 days to submit.

For the Y22s, first, the basic knowledge of digital devices and circuits was tested. In the first week, resources on Verilog HDL were provided. The first two assignments were on writing Verilog codes for basic combinational and sequential circuits, then, onto a few complex circuits like counters and rotators. Next, resources on finite-state machines (FSM), Mealy and Moore machines, state transition tables, and diagrams were provided. Towards the end of the project, the mentees were divided into teams of five and were given a practical assignment that included date converter, customized alarm clock, traffic light controller, tic-tac-toe, scientific calculator, and car parking system. The end-term report consisted of the state transition table and diagram of the FSM, the Verilog code and testbench, and its output in GTK wave.

As for the Y23s, they were first taught the truth tables, boolean expressions, implementing them with logic gates, representation as SOP and POS, and minimization by k-maps. After the first assignment, latches, flip-flops (JK, D, and T), FSMs, their state transition diagrams, and then devices like counters and multiplexers were taught. Then they were given the resources to learn Verilog HDL. The final end-term report consisted of a description of all the topics learned in the project.
